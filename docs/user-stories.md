# User Stories

## Primary Persona: Student Case Team Member

As a student preparing for a case competition, I want to rehearse judge-style questions before presentation day so that I can defend my team's recommendation with clearer evidence and fewer surprises.

Acceptance criteria:

- I can paste my case prompt, rubric, and recommendation.
- I receive likely judge questions.
- I can answer at least five questions.
- I receive a report that tells me what to improve next.

## Secondary Persona: Team Lead

As a team lead, I want a quick readiness report after practice so that I can decide what the team should fix before the next rehearsal.

Acceptance criteria:

- The report identifies strongest and weakest answers.
- The report names missed rubric criteria or weak assumptions.
- The report gives specific drills, not just generic encouragement.

## Secondary Persona: Coach Or Teaching Assistant

As a coach or TA, I want teams to arrive with a structured practice report so that live coaching time can focus on high-value feedback.

Acceptance criteria:

- The report is understandable without watching the full rehearsal.
- It separates observed evidence from interpretation.
- It does not claim to predict judge outcomes.

## User Journey

1. Team opens Case Mirror.
2. Team enters case prompt, rubric, recommendation, context, and slide notes.
3. Team reviews the generated brief and critique.
4. Team practices judge questions using typed or spoken answers.
5. Team optionally enables webcam delivery evidence.
6. Team generates the final report.
7. Team chooses the next two practice drills before presenting.
